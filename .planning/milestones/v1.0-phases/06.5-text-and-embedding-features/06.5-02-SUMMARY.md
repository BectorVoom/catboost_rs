## Decisions Made

- **NaiveBayes ttext is the unigram SC-1 oracle, not BoW ttext.** Inspecting the fixtures showed BoW dumps **two** dictionaries (BiGram + Word, per RESEARCH Pitfall 4): its `ttext` events carry BiGram token ids (up to 24) that do NOT map to the unigram `dict_ids` (0-7). The NaiveBayes path uses the Word unigram dictionary only, so its 16 `ttext` events are the correct unigram digitizer ground truth — all reproduced bit-exact. BiGram dictionary build + BoW digitization is a follow-on slice owned by the BoW calcer plan (unigram-first, RESEARCH Pitfall 4). This does NOT weaken SC-1: the unigram tokenizer/dictionary/digitizer stream — the load-bearing token→id mapping every calcer depends on — is bit-exact.
- **Oracle reads instrumented JSON dumps directly.** Text models have no `model.json` (06.5-01 deviation); the frozen `token_stream.json`/`dict_ids.json`/`ttext.json` ARE the D-07 ground truth, parsed with `serde_json` (already a cb-oracle dependency).
- **OLB resolved, not pinned.** `resolve_occurrence_lower_bound(learn_pool_size)` computes `poolSize<1000?1:5` (`options_helper.cpp:394-401`); the oracle asserts it equals 1 for the 16-row corpus rather than hard-coding the boundary (A4).
