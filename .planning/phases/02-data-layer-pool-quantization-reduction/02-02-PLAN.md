<objective>
First parity vertical slice: a raw `Pool` (owned columns + full CatBoost metadata) constructed through the ingestion trait seam, fed into the `GreedyLogSum` greedy binarizer, oracle-compared border-by-border against upstream `get_borders()`. This is the thinnest end-to-end path that proves the parity-critical algorithm — owned `Vec<f64>` in, oracle-matched borders out.

Purpose: Borders are the first place a downstream tree can be poisoned. Locking GreedyLogSum to ≤1e-5 on the frozen corpus, with all summation routed through the audited reduction primitive, is the foundational parity slice the rest of quantization builds on.
Output: `Pool`, the `IngestSource` trait + owned-`Vec` impl, `borders.rs` (GreedyLogSum), and a green `borders_oracle_test` comparing per-feature borders to the Wave-0 fixtures.
</objective>
