GAP 1 (ORD-02, FAILED) — Part 1 of 2: build the ordered split-scoring subsystem in `tree.rs`.

The previous 05-08 plan (overwritten here) assumed wiring `ordered_approx_delta_simple` into the LEAF-UPDATE alone would close ORD-02. The executor's upstream analysis (recorded in STATE.md Blockers/Concerns, 2026-06-14) proved this is WRONG: upstream ordered boosting differs from Plain primarily in the **tree STRUCTURE** — splits are scored on **per-segment ordered derivatives across the learning fold's `BodyTailArr`** (a multi-segment histogram score reduction, `scoring.cpp:746-760` `CalculateNonPairwiseScore` loops `bodyTailIdx` and sums `scoreCalcer->AddLeaf` across all segments; `scoring.cpp:301-308` `CalcStatsKernel` non-plain branch scores the tail on `SampleWeightedDerivatives` over `[BodyFinish, TailFinish)` while the body contributes `WeightedDerivatives`). `tree.rs` today has NO ordered/body-tail-segment scoring path — `greedy_tensor_search_oblivious_perturbed` scores every candidate on the WHOLE fold (`reduce_leaf_stats` over all objects, `l2_split_score`). Wiring the approximant into leaf-update alone reproduces the Plain tree structure with a perturbed leaf update — empirically ordered vs plain predictions differ max 0.70 on the e2e config, so it FAILS the ≤1e-5-vs-upstream oracle.

This plan builds the NEW ordered split-scoring subsystem (comparable in size to the existing Plain `select_level_plain`/`score_candidate`), unit-locked standalone. Plan 05-10 wires it into `train()` and locks the end-to-end multi-tree ordered oracle. WR-01 (dead `sum_weights`) is cleaned up here since this plan owns `tree.rs` + the ordered approximant.

Purpose: deliver the structural heart of ORD-02 — per-segment ordered split scoring — as a standalone, unit-locked subsystem so 05-10 can drive it from the train loop without re-deriving the algorithm.
Output: `greedy_tensor_search_oblivious_ordered` (+ helpers) in `tree.rs`, its sibling unit test, the WR-01 cleanup in `boosting.rs`, and the lib re-export.
