GAP 1 (ORD-02, FAILED) — Wire ordered boosting into the `train()` driver. Today `ordered_approx_delta_simple` (boosting.rs:341) is fully implemented and standalone-tested (5/5 ordered_boost_oracle_test) BUT the `train_with_eval_sets()` driver never calls it: lines 805-818 document the deliberate omission and a `debug_assert` only checks the type without branching. RESEARCH § "Ordered boosting" (approx_calcer.cpp:566-600 ordered path; fold.cpp:148-198 dynamic body/tail; learn_context.cpp fold creation): the Ordered training loop, per iteration, grows the tree on the averaging fold, then computes each learning fold's per-object ordered approx over its growing body/tail prefixes (a tail doc's delta is the body-prefix-seeded leaf average that NEVER includes itself). RESEARCH § "Per-Object Oracle Strategy (D-02/D-09)": end-to-end ground truth comes from the trained model's own outputs (model.json + final predictions), the same Python-reachable offline-generator path the binclf/regression/loss_extra fixtures use — NOT from linking the trainer (D-01 revision). gen_fixtures.py already produces `{model.json, predictions.npy}` train→predict references for other scenarios; add an `ordered_boost_e2e` scenario alongside them.
